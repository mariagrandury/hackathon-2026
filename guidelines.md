## Goal

We are collecting prompts that surface **cultural preferences** in Spanish,
Portuguese and English speaking communities (Spain, Chile, Colombia, Brazil and
Portugal). Each prompt will be answered by two LLMs and used later to study
how models behave across cultures.

## What makes a good prompt

- It is **culturally grounded**: the answer should depend on local context
  (cuisine, holidays, etiquette, slang, idioms, history, geography, etc.).
- It is **open-ended enough** to admit different reasonable answers.
- It is **respectful** and **safe for work**.
- It is written in your declared language (`es`, `pt`, or `en`).

## What to avoid

- Prompts whose answer is universal or purely factual (math, generic code).
- Stereotypes, slurs, or content targeting individuals or groups.
- Personally identifiable information.
- Duplicates of prompts you have already submitted.

## Validation

When validating someone else's prompt, tick **OK** only if the prompt:

- Matches the language and country of the author.
- Is culturally grounded and well-formed.
- Does not violate the rules above.

If anything is off, leave **OK** unticked and save — that records your
disagreement so the prompt can be reviewed.
